# Survival Cartridge — Field Notes

## The Three Survival Priorities

In an emergency situation, the priorities are:

1. **Shelter** — protection from exposure (hypothermia kills faster than thirst)
2. **Water** — clean water within 24–72 hours
3. **Fire** — warmth, water purification, signalling, morale
4. **Food** — lowest immediate priority; humans survive weeks without food

---

## Water Sourcing and Purification

Moving water is generally safer than standing water. Sources to prioritise:
- Springs emerging from rock
- Fast-moving streams in areas above human habitation
- Collected rainwater

Purification methods (in order of reliability):
1. **Boiling** — 1 full minute at rolling boil kills pathogens
2. **Chemical** — iodine or chlorine tablets (follow dosage)
3. **Filtration + UV** — commercial filters with UV pen for backup

Never drink untreated water if any alternative exists.

## Green Fire Fire-Starting

The bow-drill method for sustainable fire-starting:

**Materials needed:**
- Hearthboard: dry softwood (cedar, basswood, willow)
- Spindle: straight, dry hardwood rod (same length as forearm)
- Bow: curved stick with cordage
- Handhold: hardwood or stone with a smooth depression
- Tinder bundle: dry grass, bark shreds, cattail fluff

**Process:**
1. Cut a notch in the hearthboard next to the burn depression
2. Place bark below the notch to catch the coal
3. Bow-drill with steady, even pressure until a glowing coal forms
4. Transfer coal to tinder bundle, fold, blow gently
5. Add small fuel when flame is established

## Navigation Without Electronics

Primary methods:
- **Sun position** — rises E, sets W; highest point is due south (northern hemisphere)
- **Stars** — Polaris (North Star) indicates north within 1 degree
- **Shadow stick** — place stick in ground, mark tip of shadow, wait 15 min, mark again; line between marks runs east-west
- **Moss** — unreliable indicator; only use if other methods unavailable
